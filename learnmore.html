Learn more about addiciton to video games

Learn More!! We can help!

While video games can be fun and improve skills like problem-solving and coordination, excessive gaming can seriously harm your mental, emotional, and physical health. Video game addiction often begins as a way to escape stress or problems, but it can quickly turn into a harmful cycle. Common signs include withdrawal symptoms like anxiety, irritability, and neglecting school, work, or relationships.


Teenagers, especially males aged 12–16, are most at risk due to long gaming hours, multiplayer games, and daily play. Addiction can lead to increased depression, social anxiety, inattention, poor sleep, and physical issues like bad posture, eye strain, and lack of fitness. These effects worsen over time if gaming is not managed.


To stop the addiction, it’s important to set screen time limits, take regular breaks, avoid late-night gaming, and balance play with real-life activities like exercise and face-to-face time with friends and family. If gaming starts to take over your life or affect your well-being, don’t wait and seek support from a trusted adult, school counselor, or mental health professional. You’re not alone, and recovery is possible with the right help and healthy habits.
